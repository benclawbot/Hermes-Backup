# Wireless Infrastructure Investment Cycle

> [!info] Summary
> Wireless infrastructure spending cycles are highly cyclical, driven by technology transitions (3G/4G/5G) and spectrum auction timing. 4G investment peaked globally ~2015; 5G peaked ~2021-2023 with C-band and mmWave deployments. Carrier capex typically runs 12-18% of revenue for mature operators. The current trough (2024-2026) is transitioning from 5G buildout to 5G SA core and private network deployments. Next catalyst: 5G Advanced (Release 18+) from 2025 and 6G research/pre-deployment from 2028.

## Definition

Wireless infrastructure investment cycles refer to the pattern of concentrated capital expenditure by telecom operators during cellular network technology deployments, followed by lower-intensity spending periods during network optimization and before the next generation. Key metrics: carrier capex as % of revenue (typically 12-18%), network opex per gigabyte (declining with each generation due to efficiency gains), and 5G coverage population percent.

## Context and origin

The cellular infrastructure investment model was established in the 2G era and has repeated with each generation: Phase 1 (Launch, Years 1-3): Heavy capex for new spectrum licenses, new core and radio networks. Phase 2 (Buildout, Years 3-7): Continued investment in coverage and capacity, infrastructure expansion. Phase 3 (Trough, Years 7-10): Minimal new investment, focus on opex optimization. Phase 4 (Next Generation Launch): Spectrum acquisitions, trial networks, early deployment.

**The 4G cycle:** Global 4G investment peaked ~2015-2016 as operators completed LTE coverage buildouts. The trough extended from 2017-2019 before 5G trials began.

**The 5G cycle:** 5G investment began 2019-2020 with sub-6GHz deployments. The US C-band auction (2021, $45B+ raised for FCC) was the peak of the 5G investment cycle. Global 5G investment peaked 2021-2023. By 2024, most major markets have 5G coverage; investment is shifting to capacity, densification, and 5G Standalone (SA) core.

## Mechanisms / characteristics / details

**Geographic variation:** China led the 5G cycle — operators invested heavily in 2020-2023, building 3M+ base stations (vs ~100K in the US and ~50K in Europe as of 2023). Chinese 5G capex peaked earlier and is declining faster. The trough is therefore more advanced in China than in Western markets.

**Vendor landscape:** Huawei, Ericsson, Nokia, and Samsung are the primary telecom infrastructure vendors globally. Huawei (~30% revenue share) dominates globally but has been restricted in the US, Australia, UK, and some European markets due to security concerns. Ericsson and Nokia have gained share in Western markets as a result. Samsung has ~3-5% global share but is significant in South Korea and is growing in the US.

**Private 5G network cycle:** The emerging private 5G network market represents a new investment cycle distinct from carrier networks. Enterprises (factories, ports, stadiums, logistics hubs) are deploying private LTE/5G networks. This is creating a new customer segment for infrastructure vendors: systems integrators (Ericsson Private 5G, Nokia DAC, Samsung Networks) vs traditional carrier relationships.

**Open RAN disruption:** O-RAN (Open Radio Access Network) architecture separates hardware and software, enabling multi-vendor RAN deployments. This could disrupt the traditional Ericsson/Nokia/Huawei oligopoly by enabling new entrants (Open RAN vendors like Mavenir, Rakuten Symphony, Parallel Wireless). Adoption is still early (5-10% of RAN deployments) but growing.

## Nuances critiques limits

**ROI challenge:** Operators have struggled to generate ROI from 5G investments. The primary monetization thesis (new revenue from B2B enterprise services, network slicing, URLLC) has been slower than expected. Consumer pricing hasn't increased meaningfully. This creates capex caution heading into the next cycle.

**Sharing arrangements:** To manage capex, operators increasingly share infrastructure (tower sharing, passive sharing, active sharing). In India, Bharti Airtel and Vodafone Idea share 4G networks. In Europe, there are multiple network sharing agreements. This reduces total industry capex vs independent buildouts.

**6G research budgets:** Major operators and vendors are beginning 6G R&D spending (anticipated commercial launch ~2030). This is currently a small fraction of capex but will grow. Standards work is expected to begin 3GPP Release 21 (2026+).

**The 5G Advanced cycle:** 3GPP Release 18 (5G Advanced) is being deployed from 2024. This is not a new generation but incremental improvements to 5G (AI/ML for network optimization, XR support, expanded IoT). This represents a smaller but meaningful upgrade cycle for infrastructure vendors.

## Links and implications

[[Wireless Infrastructure Investment Cycle]] is driven by the [[5G Infrastructure Market]] rollout phases. The cycle funds [[Small Cell Deployment]] (the densification phase of 5G). Wireless infrastructure traffic ultimately flows through [[Subsea Cable Networks]] and [[Hyperscale Data Center Operators]] — the investment in wireless infrastructure is only valuable if the backhaul and cloud infrastructure can carry the traffic. [[Edge Computing Infrastructure]] and private 5G networks represent the B2B revenue model that could justify continued investment. The cycle also drives semiconductor content in [[Data Center Networking Chips]] (for network equipment) and [[Compound Semiconductors]] (for RF components in infrastructure radios).
