# Lot 5: Wireless & Connectivity — Map of Concepts

## Pages

| Page | Summary |
|------|---------|
| [[5G Infrastructure Market]] | The 5G infrastructure market is ~$30B annually, transitioning from NSA to SA architecture. Key semiconductor content: RF front end, massive MIMO transceivers, and baseband processors. Huawei, Ericsson, and Nokia dominate globally, with US export controls reshaping Western market share. |
| [[Antenna Technologies Mass MIMO]] | Massive MIMO (Multiple Input Multiple Output) uses dozens to hundreds of antenna elements to dramatically increase spectral efficiency and throughput. 64T64R (64 transmit, 64 receive) is the standard configuration for 5G sub-6GHz macro cells. The technology is the core driver of 5G's capacity improvement over 4G, requiring advanced RF front-end modules and compound semiconductor power amplifiers. |
| [[Bluetooth LE Audio]] | Bluetooth LE Audio, finalized in Bluetooth 2020 specs, introduced LC3 codec (better quality at lower bitrate), audio sharing, and support for hearing aids. The new Auracast broadcast audio feature enables location-based audio experiences. Key players in Bluetooth silicon: Nordic Semiconductor (dominant in IoT), Qualcomm, MediaTek, and Cypress (Infineon). |
| [[Cellular Baseband Modems]] | Cellular baseband processors handle the modem functions in mobile devices — encoding, decoding, and managing radio protocols. Qualcomm dominates the smartphone baseband market with ~60% share. MediaTek is the primary second source. Intel sold its baseband business to Apple in 2019. |
| [[RF Front End Modules]] | RF front end modules handle signal amplification, filtering, and switching between antenna and baseband. For 5G, the RFFE content per phone increased substantially with support for more bands and carrier aggregation. Key players: Skyworks Solutions, Qorvo, Broadcom, and Murata. |
| [[Satellite Communications Chips]] | Satellite communications chips enable direct-to-device connectivity for coverage in underserved and remote areas. Apple's Emergency SOS via satellite and Qualcomm's Snapdragon Satellite are early implementations. Globalstar, Iridium, and SpaceX's Starlink are the primary satellite operators. Key chip suppliers: Qualcomm, Apple (custom), Globalstar, and semiconductor players investing in satellite modem silicon. |
| [[Small Cell Deployment]] | Small cells are low-power radio access nodes for dense urban and enterprise 5G coverage. They address the capacity and coverage gaps that macro cells cannot solve in high-traffic areas. Key players: Ericsson, Nokia, Samsung, and neutral host providers like Dense Air and Airspan. |
| [[WiFi Chip Market]] | The WiFi chip market is ~$15B annually, segmented by standard (WiFi 6, 6E, 7) and application (smartphone, PC, IoT, router). Broadcom, Qualcomm, MediaTek, and Intel are the primary suppliers for consumer WiFi. For enterprise and IoT, Quantenna, Celeno, and others compete. The演进 to WiFi 7 (802.11be) is driving a new upgrade cycle. |
| [[Wireless Infrastructure Investment Cycle]] | Wireless infrastructure spending cycles track regulatory auction calendars and operator capex budgets. 4G cycle peaked ~2015; 5G cycle accelerated from 2019 with C-band and mmWave deployments. Private 5G networks represent a new growth vector for enterprise adoption. |
| [[mmWave Radar Technologies]] | mmWave radar (24-100 GHz) serves both 5G telecommunications and automotive sensing. In 5G, mmWave enables ultra-wideband channels for fixed wireless access and dense urban hotspots. In automotive, 77 GHz radar is standard for ADAS. The overlap between telecom and sensing creates opportunities for compound semiconductor players like Qorvo and Skyworks. |
