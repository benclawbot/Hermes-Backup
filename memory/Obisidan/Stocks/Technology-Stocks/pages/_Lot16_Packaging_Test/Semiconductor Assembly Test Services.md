# Semiconductor Assembly Test Services

> [!info] Summary
> Semiconductor assembly and test services (OSAT — Outsourced Semiconductor Assembly and Test) handle the backend chip manufacturing steps of packaging and testing that integrated device manufacturers (IDMs) and fabless companies increasingly outsource. The market is dominated by ASE Group, Amkor Technology, and JCET, with Taiwan and China-based players controlling most volume.

## Definition

Semiconductor manufacturing has two phases: frontend (wafer fabrication in fabs) and backend (assembly and test). Backend includes: wafer dicing (cutting individual chips from the wafer), die attach (mounting the chip on a leadframe, substrate, or interposer), wire bonding or flip-chip interconnect, molding or encapsulation, marking and lead trimming, and final test (checking speed grade, functionality, and burning in infant mortality).

OSAT companies provide these services to IDMs (who outsource excess capacity or specialized packaging) and fabless companies (who have no internal packaging capability). The industry grew as fabless companies emerged in the 1980s and 1990s and needed assembly/test partners.

## Context and origin

The OSAT model emerged in the 1980s as semiconductor companies began outsourcing backend to specialized service providers. ASE (Advanced Semiconductor Engineering) in Taiwan became the model, demonstrating that a pure-play assembly/test company could achieve scale and quality superior to integrated manufacturers.

The 2000s-2010s saw massive growth in OSAT driven by the fabless boom (Qualcomm, Broadcom, NVIDIA, AMD all used OSAT) and the shift of IDMs toward fab-light models. During the 2021-2022 semiconductor shortage, OSAT companies were capacity-constrained, highlighting their strategic importance.

## Mechanisms / characteristics / details

The assembly/test market is highly fragmented by packaging type. Standard wire-bond packages (QFN, SOIC, TSSOP) are low-margin, high-volume. Advanced packages (flip-chip BGA, fcBGA, Fan-Out) command higher margins and require significant capital investment.

[[OSAT Provider Analysis ASE Amkor]] covers the major players in detail. The link to [[Test Equipment Market Teradyne]] is important because test equipment (memory testers, SoC testers, RF testers) is a major capex component for OSAT companies.

Advanced packaging (see [[Advanced Packaging Technology Overview]]) is the growth driver. Chiplets ([[Chiplet Based Design Ecosystem]]), 2.5D interposers, and Fan-Out ([[Fan Out Wafer Level Packaging]]) require sophisticated assembly capabilities that are increasingly differentiated.

## Nuances critiques limits

The China risk is significant for OSAT. Chinese government investment has created domestic OSAT competitors (JT Star, Tianshui Hua, JCET subsidiary) that are rapidly scaling. This creates both competitive pressure and geopolitical risk for non-Chinese OSAT customers.

The advanced packaging transition creates opportunity but requires massive capital investment. OSAT companies that cannot afford to invest in Fan-Out, 2.5D, and chiplet assembly capability may be relegated to commodity packaging.

ASE and Amkor have the most advanced capability. JCET (through acquiring STATS ChipPAC) is also competitive. The gap between top-tier and second-tier OSAT is widening.
