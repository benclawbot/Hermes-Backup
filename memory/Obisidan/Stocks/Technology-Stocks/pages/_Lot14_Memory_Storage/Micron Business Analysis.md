# Micron Business Analysis

> [!info] Summary
> Micron Technology is the only major US-based memory semiconductor company, producing DRAM, NAND, and NOR flash memory. Micron's strategic position is distinctive as the only non-Korean memory manufacturer at scale, making it both a critical supplier to US technology companies and a geopolitical asset in the semiconductor supply chain, though its financial performance has been more volatile than Korean competitors.

## Definition

Micron Technology designs and manufactures DRAM, NAND flash, and NOR flash memory chips. The company operates DRAM fabs in Idaho and Virginia (USA), and NAND fabs in Utah and Singapore. Micron is the only major memory manufacturer incorporated in the United States, giving it a unique position in US national security supply chain considerations.

Key products include: compute DRAM (DDR4/DDR5 for servers and PCs), mobile LPDDR, graphics GDDR (for GPUs), HBM (in development), 3D NAND for SSDs and embedded, and NOR flash for automotive and industrial applications.

## Context and origin

Micron was founded in 1978 as a semiconductor design company and built its first fab in the 1980s. The company survived the brutal DRAM troughs of the 1990s and early 2000s through a combination of government support (during the 1980s Japan-Korea DRAM competition), technology partnerships, and operational discipline.

The 2013 acquisition of Elpida Memory (Japan's last DRAM manufacturer) and the Rexchip DRAM fab in Taiwan gave Micron critical mass in DRAM. The 40%+ market share in DRAM that this acquisition enabled has been the foundation of Micron's business.

## Mechanisms / characteristics / details

Micron's competitive position is weaker than Samsung or Hynix in DRAM scale but stronger in certain niches. Micron is the primary GDDR6 supplier for gaming GPUs (competing with Samsung), and is the only US-based HBM developer (competing with Samsung and Hynix).

Micron has been slower than Samsung and Hynix to reach competitive HBM production. The company's HBM3 qualifications with AI GPU customers have been delayed relative to Hynix's HBM3 supply. Micron has set a goal of achieving HBM competitive leadership by 2025-2026.

## Nuances critiques limits

Micron's US incorporation is both its greatest strength and a strategic constraint. As a US company, Micron benefits from US government support and is a preferred supplier for US defense and government applications. However, this also means Micron is subject to US export controls, preventing it from serving certain international customers (particularly Chinese companies on restricted lists).

The CHIPS Act provides Micron with significant potential US government subsidies for new US fab construction, which could improve its cost competitiveness against Korean competitors. However, the bureaucratic complexity of CHIPS Act funding has slowed actual construction timelines.

Micron's financial performance has historically been more cyclical than Korean competitors, partly because it lacks the diversified electronics business that cushions Samsung and partially because its smaller scale gives less operational leverage.
