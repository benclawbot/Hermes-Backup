# Hynix Memory Business

> [!info] Summary
> SK Hynix is the world's second-largest memory semiconductor company, dominant in DRAM and a major NAND producer, with a strategic emphasis on high-bandwidth memory (HBM) for AI training. Hynix's early and sustained investment in HBM positioned it as the primary supplier to NVIDIA's AI training GPUs, creating a strategically critical position in the AI infrastructure supply chain.

## Definition

SK Hynix (formed from the 2012 merger of Hynix Semiconductor and SK Telecom's acquisition) manufactures DRAM and NAND flash memory chips used in smartphones, PCs, servers, and consumer electronics. The company is headquartered in Icheon, South Korea, and operates memory fabs in Korea and China. After acquiring Intel's NAND business in 2021, Hynix became the world's second-largest NAND producer.

Key products include: standard DDR4/DDR5 DRAM for PCs and servers, LPDDR for mobile, HBM2/HBM3 for AI GPUs, and 3D NAND flash for SSDs and embedded storage.

## Context and origin

Hynix was formed from the bankruptcy reorganization of Korea's Hyundai Electronics in 2001, creating a company that competed in DRAM through the difficult early 2000s when DRAM prices frequently fell below manufacturing cost. The company survived multiple restructuring cycles and eventually became SK Hynix when SK Telecom acquired a controlling stake in 2012.

The 2021 acquisition of Intel's NAND business (Solidigm) gave Hynix scale in NAND it previously lacked, creating a more balanced memory portfolio. The NAND integration has been challenging, with market conditions being poor in 2022-2023.

Hynix's strategic masterstroke was its early investment in HBM (high-bandwidth memory). Starting HBM development before the AI boom gave Hynix a manufacturing learning curve advantage that competitors are still trying to close. Hynix is currently the sole HBM3 supplier for NVIDIA's H100 and is qualification-supplying HBM3E for newer GPU generations.

## Mechanisms / characteristics / details

Hynix's HBM leadership is its most strategically valuable asset. HBM provides the massive memory bandwidth that GPU accelerators need for AI training, and the close collaboration with NVIDIA (including co-design of HBM specifications for future GPU generations) creates a relationship that is difficult for Samsung or Micron to replicate quickly.

Hynix's [[DRAM Memory Market]] position is #2 globally in DRAM, behind Samsung and ahead of Micron. The NAND business (Solidigm) is the #2 NAND producer. The combined scale gives Hynix leverage with equipment and materials suppliers.

The [[High Bandwidth Memory HBM]] page covers the technology in detail. The link to [[AI Accelerator Chips]] as the primary HBM consumer is the key demand relationship.

## Nuances critiques limits

Hynix's NAND business (Solidigm) has struggled with profitability in the post-acquisition period. The NAND market downturn of 2022-2023 made the integration challenging, and Hynix has had to write down Intel NAND-related assets.

Samsung Memory Business is Hynix's primary DRAM competitor and is investing aggressively to regain HBM leadership. If Samsung closes the HBM technology gap, Hynix's HBM pricing power and margins could erode.

The China risk is significant. CXMT (ChangXin Memory Technologies) is building domestic Chinese DRAM capability. While CXMT is years behind in process technology, Chinese government support could accelerate its progress.
