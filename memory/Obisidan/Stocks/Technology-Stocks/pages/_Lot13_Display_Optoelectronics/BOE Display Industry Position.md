# BOE Display Industry Position

> [!info] Summary
> BOE Technology Group (also known as BOE) is the world's largest display panel manufacturer by capacity and volume, having surpassed Samsung Display and LG Display through massive government-subsidized expansion in LCD and rapid growth in OLED. BOE is the leading supplier of LCD panels for TVs and laptops, a growing OLED supplier for smartphones, and is aggressively investing in IT OLED capacity to compete with Samsung Display for Apple and other premium customers.

## Definition

BOE Technology Group Co., Ltd. (Beijing Oriental Electronics) is a Chinese state-supported display manufacturer producing LCD panels, OLED panels, and other光电 components. The company is headquartered in Beijing with manufacturing facilities across China. It is the largest single display manufacturer by volume and is rapidly growing in OLED, supplying panels to Apple, Samsung, Huawei, and other brands.

BOE's products span: LCD TV panels (the company's historical strength), LCD IT panels (monitors, laptops), small-medium OLED panels (smartphones, watches), and large-area OLED panels (TVs, beginning to ship). The company is investing heavily in Gen 8.7 OLED fab capacity for IT products.

## Context and origin

BOE was founded in 1993 as a state-owned enterprise and grew steadily as a CRT and early LCD manufacturer. The company's modern expansion began in the 2010s when Chinese government policy actively supported display manufacturing as a strategic industry. BOE received tens of billions of dollars in subsidies, land grants, and policy support to build leading-edge LCD and OLED fabs.

The Gen 10.5 LCD fab in Hefei (built with government support) gave BOE cost leadership in large-area TV panels by allowing more efficient切割 (cutting) of 65-inch and 75-inch panels from larger substrates. This cost advantage accelerated BOE's rise to LCD leadership.

Apple's addition of BOE as an iPhone OLED supplier in 2022 was a watershed moment, validating BOE's OLED manufacturing capability after years of being considered behind Samsung Display and LG Display in OLED quality.

## Mechanisms / characteristics / details

BOE's competitive advantage is primarily cost, enabled by Chinese government subsidies, large fab scale, and lower labor costs. The company's Gen 10.5 LCD fabs are among the most cost-efficient in the world for large-area TV panels. This has allowed BOE to drive LCD panel prices down globally, squeezing Samsung Display and LG Display out of LCD profitability.

In OLED, BOE is growing rapidly but still lags Samsung Display in yield and quality, particularly for Apple's iPhone Pro panels. BOE supplies standard iPhone OLED panels and has received approvals for some Pro panel production, but Samsung Display remains the primary iPhone Pro supplier.

The IT OLED investment is strategic. BOE is building Gen 8.7 OLED capacity in Beijing and Chongqing targeting laptop and monitor panels. The goal is to win Apple MacBook OLED orders as Apple migrates away from LCD in laptops. This represents the next major competitive battleground between BOE and Samsung Display.

The link to [[China Semiconductor Policy]] is important because BOE's display expansion was part of the same state industrial policy that funded semiconductor manufacturing. The skills and supply chains developed in display manufacturing have partially transferred to semiconductor ambition.

## Nuances critiques limits

BOE faces significant geopolitical risk. US export controls on semiconductor manufacturing equipment could eventually restrict BOE's ability to build leading-edge OLED capacity. ASML's EUV machines (needed for the most advanced OLED backplane manufacturing) are already restricted from sale to Chinese companies.

Samsung Display and LG Display are fighting back aggressively, shifting focus to OLED where they retain technological leadership. The LCD market has become a commodity business that Chinese producers dominate, but the higher-margin future is OLED.

BOE's OLED yields and consistency still lag Samsung Display. Even with similar equipment, the manufacturing learning, material formulations, and process knowledge积累 take time to replicate.
