# Mobile Chipset Market

> [!info] Summary
> The mobile SoC market is a duopoly in Android (Qualcomm and MediaTek) with Apple as a captive sole-source for iPhones. Qualcomm dominates premium Android (Snapdragon 8 series) while MediaTek leads in mid-range and emerging markets. The market is evolving with Apple's internal 5G modem transition, 5G infrastructure buildout driving upgrade cycles, and competitive pressure from Google's Tensor custom silicon.

## Definition

A mobile SoC (system on chip) integrates: application processor cores (ARM-based), GPU, cellular modem (4G/5G), WiFi/Bluetooth/FM radio, GPS, image signal processor (ISP), neural processing unit (NPU), audio codec, power management ICs, and sometimes the RF front end.

The market includes: premium SoCs ($50-120 ASP, Snapdragon 8 series, Apple A-series), mid-range SoCs ($25-50 ASP, Snapdragon 7 series, MediaTek Dimensity 8000 series), and entry SoCs ($8-25 ASP, MediaTek Helio G series, Qualcomm Snapdragon 6 series).

## Context and origin

The smartphone SoC market emerged with Qualcomm's Snapdragon (2007), TI OMAP, Samsung Exynos, NVIDIA Tegra, and others. Competition has since narrowed to Qualcomm, MediaTek, Samsung (self captive), Apple (self captive), and HiSilicon (Huawei, now US-sanctioned).

The transition from 4G LTE to 5G (starting ~2019) drove significant SoC replacement cycles as consumers upgraded to 5G phones. The mid-range 5G transition (5G in $250-400 phones) has been a major volume driver for MediaTek.

## Nuances critiques limits

Qualcomm's position in premium Android is strong but faces pressure from MediaTek's Dimensity 9000+ series closing the performance gap at lower price points. Apple's iPhone uses Apple's own A-series chips, making iPhone SoC supply a non-competitive dynamic.

Samsung's Exynos SoCs (used in some Galaxy S flagships outside North States) have underperformed Qualcomm alternatives in benchmark and efficiency, creating brand damage that Samsung is addressing by standardizing on Snapdragon for flagship Galaxy phones globally.
