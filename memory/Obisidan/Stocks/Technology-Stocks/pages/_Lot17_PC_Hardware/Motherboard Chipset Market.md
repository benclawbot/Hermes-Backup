# Motherboard Chipset Market

> [!info] Summary
> PC motherboard chipsets are platform controllers that manage I/O connectivity, expandability, and connectivity for CPU platforms. Intel and AMD each design their own chipsets for their respective platforms, while third-party suppliers (ASMedia for USB, Realtek for audio/LAN) provide peripheral controllers. The market is mature and stable, with platform roadmaps tightly coupled to CPU architecture transitions.

## Definition

A PC motherboard chipset manages: PCIe lanes from the CPU (connecting GPU, NVMe storage, network cards), USB ports (via platform controller hub or CPU-integrated USB), SATA/M.2 storage interfaces, audio codec, LAN Ethernet, BIOS/UEFI firmware, and power delivery to CPU/RAM.

In Intel platforms, the chipset is a separate chip (PCH — Platform Controller Hub). In AMD platforms, the I/O die of the chiplet-based design serves as the chipset. The chipset determines platform features: PCIe version, USB version, storage capabilities, and overclocking support.

## Context and origin

The motherboard chipset market evolved from the 1990s "northbridge/southbridge" architecture (separate memory controller northbridge and I/O southbridge) to today's integrated architectures where CPUs contain the memory controller and most I/O. The remaining chipset functions (fewer PCIe lanes, USB, SATA) are handled by a single PCH chip.

ASMedia (a subsidiary of ASUS) is the primary third-party chipset supplier for USB and SATA controllers. Realtek supplies audio codecs and Ethernet controllers. These companies represent the sub-supply chain within the motherboard ecosystem.

## Nuances critiques limits

The desktop PC motherboard market is mature and declining as more computing moves to mobile devices. Enthusiast motherboards (gaming, workstation-class) command premium pricing and margins, but unit volumes are small.

The ultra-small form factor (Mini-ITX) market has grown as home theater PC (HTPC) and compact gaming builds have become more popular. This creates demand for higher-density, premium-priced small motherboards.

ASUS, ASRock, Gigabyte, and MSI are the major motherboard ODMs/OEMs. ASUSTeK Computer (ASUS) is the brand leader in enthusiast motherboards.
