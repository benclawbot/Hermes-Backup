# PC Component Supply Chain

> [!info] Summary
> The PC component supply chain encompasses CPU suppliers (Intel, AMD), GPU suppliers (NVIDIA, AMD), memory (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron), storage (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron, Kioxia, WD), motherboard chipsets (Intel, AMD), power supplies, and cooling. The chain experienced severe constraints in 2020-2022 and has since normalized, with geopolitical risks (Taiwan, China) increasingly influencing sourcing decisions.

## Definition

The PC supply chain has several tiers: Tier 1 component suppliers (Intel, AMD, NVIDIA, Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron), Tier 2 subsystem suppliers (ASUS, ASRock, MSI for motherboards; Seasonic, Corsair for PSUs; Noctua, be quiet! for cooling), Tier 3 peripheral and system integrators (Dell, HP, Lenovo, custom PC builders).

Key components for PC performance: CPU (Intel or AMD), GPU (discrete for gaming/workstation, integrated for mainstream), DRAM (DDR5 or DDR4), storage (NVMe SSD), and thermal management.

## Context and origin

The PC supply chain was globalized over decades, with manufacturing concentrated in Taiwan (TSMC for chips, Foxconn for assembly), China (component manufacturing and assembly), and South Korea (memory). This concentration created efficiency but also vulnerability.

The 2020-2022 semiconductor shortage exposed this vulnerability. GPU prices spiked 2-3x above MSRP, CPU supply was constrained, and SSD/DRAM prices rose. The shortage was followed by a rapid inventory correction in 2022-2023 that created oversupply.

## Nuances critiques limits

The channel inventory problem: PC component distributors hold 4-8 weeks of inventory typically. During shortages, channel inventory can balloon to 12+ weeks. When demand drops (as it did in 2022-2023), this channel inventory must be消化ed before new orders recover.

The custom PC builder market (representing 10-15% of PC sales by value) is served by distributors (Arrow, Avnet, Ingram Micro) and e-tailers (Amazon, Newegg, B&H). This market is more exposed to component pricing volatility than the OEM market.
