# PC Gaming Hardware Market

> [!info] Summary
> The PC gaming hardware market is a premium segment encompassing discrete GPUs, gaming monitors, mechanical keyboards, gaming mice, and high-performance PC components. NVIDIA dominates discrete GPU gaming market share, followed by AMD. The segment has proven resilient despite console competition and benefits from the streaming and content creation ecosystem.

## Definition

PC gaming hardware includes: discrete graphics cards (the dominant revenue category), gaming monitors (high-refresh-rate, low-latency displays), mechanical keyboards with Cherry MX or equivalent switches, gaming mice with high-DPI sensors, PC cases with airflow optimization, gaming chairs, and audio headsets.

The market is measured in billions of dollars annually. Discrete GPUs are the largest segment at $40-50B+ annually. The enthusiast segment (>$1000 GPU purchases) represents a small fraction of unit volume but a large fraction of revenue.

## Context and origin

The PC gaming market emerged in the 1990s alongside 3D graphics accelerators (3dfx Voodoo, NVIDIA RIVA TNT, ATI Rage). The industry evolved from software-only 3D rendering to hardware-accelerated polygon and texture pipelines, culminating in the modern era of ray-tracing GPUs.

The COVID-19 pandemic drove a significant gaming PC demand surge (2020-2021) as people stayed home, followed by a normalization in 2022-2023. The launch of NVIDIA's RTX 40-series and AMD's RDNA 3 GPUs in 2022-2023 maintained revenue even as unit volumes declined.

## Nuances critiques limits

Crypto currency mining was a significant demand driver in 2017-2018 and 2020-2021, distorting demand patterns. When crypto mining profitability declined, GPU inventory accumulated in the channel.

The console vs PC competition: consoles (PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo Switch) compete for gaming time and budget but do not directly substitute for high-refresh-rate PC gaming, which remains superior for competitive gaming and certain genres.

Steam hardware surveys show the majority of PC gamers use mid-range GPUs, but GPU manufacturers' revenue depends heavily on the enthusiast segment.
