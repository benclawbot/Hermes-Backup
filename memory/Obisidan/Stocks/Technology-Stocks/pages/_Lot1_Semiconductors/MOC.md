# Lot 1: Semiconductors — Map of Concepts

> [!info] Overview
> 9 pages covering the foundational semiconductor ecosystem: industry structure, business models, equipment, materials, and core technologies.

## Pages

| Page | Summary |
|------|---------|
| [[Semiconductor Industry Overview]] | The semiconductor industry designs and manufactures the chips that power all modern computing, with the sector generating over $600B in annual revenue and serving as a critical bottleneck in the global technology supply chain. |
| [[Foundry Business Model]] | The foundry business model separates chip design from fabrication, allowing foundries to achieve scale economies that individual fabless designers could never attain alone, with TSMC's model becoming the industry standard that enabled the modern semiconductor ecosystem. |
| [[Semiconductor Equipment Makers]] | Semiconductor equipment makers supply the capital goods—deposition systems, etch tools, lithography scanners, metrology instruments—that chipmakers use to manufacture silicon devices; the sector concentrates around a handful of irreplaceable suppliers. |
| [[Advanced Packaging Technologies]] | Advanced packaging refers to new ways of assembling chips beyond traditional single-die approaches. As Moore's Law scaling slows, packaging innovations have become critical for continuing performance improvements. Key technologies include 2.5D interposers, 3D stacking, fan-out wafer-level packaging, and chiplets. |
| [[EUV Lithography Systems]] | EUV lithography uses 13.5nm extreme ultraviolet light to pattern chips at resolutions impossible with deep ultraviolet light. ASML holds a monopoly on EUV systems, which cost over $150M each and are critical for manufacturing chips below 7nm. |
| [[Compound Semiconductors]] | Compound semiconductors combine elements from multiple columns of the periodic table — typically III-V compounds like gallium arsenide (GaAs), gallium nitride (GaN), and indium phosphide (InP). These materials offer advantages over silicon including higher electron mobility, wider bandgap, and ability to emit light, making them essential for RF power, LED lighting, photonics, and high-frequency applications. |
| [[Chip Design Software EDA]] | Electronic Design Automation (EDA) software provides the tools chip designers use to create, verify, and optimize complex integrated circuits before manufacturing. The $15B EDA market is dominated by Synopsys, Cadence Design Systems, and Siemens EDA, which collectively control ~80% of revenue and serve as essential infrastructure for virtually every semiconductor company globally. |
| [[Fabless vs IDM Comparison]] | The comparison between fabless companies (which design chips but outsource manufacturing) and integrated device manufacturers (IDMs which design and manufacture internally) reveals fundamental trade-offs in capital allocation, innovation speed, and strategic flexibility that shape competitive dynamics across the semiconductor industry. |
| [[Memory Technologies DRAM NAND]] | DRAM and NAND flash constitute the two dominant memory technologies, together comprising ~$100B market; DRAM serves main memory roles requiring random access speed while NAND serves storage with higher density and lower cost per bit. |
